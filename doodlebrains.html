Doodle Brains

Exploring the idle creativity of Neural Networks


CountSheep: Can Androids Count Electric Sheep?

(github)

The MNIST database of handwritten numbers is perhaps the most prolific dataset in machine learning. Given a set of handwritten numbers and the digit they are depicting, a neural network can learn to read human written digits it has never seen before with astounding accuracy.

As our maiden voyage into implementing doodle brains, I wanted to turn this basic task in on itself; bend the formulaic task of categorization into a more creative one. This neural network, "CountSheep", is trained to take in a hand drawn digit and draw the next digit. Feeding this depiction back into the network again allows it to spiral into abstraction and introspection, dipping into nonsense and suddenly pulling back semblances of meaning.
